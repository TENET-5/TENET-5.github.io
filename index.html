They Killed My Family.
Then They Prosecuted Me.

A Canadian veteran reported foreign interference inside the military. What followed was retaliation, tragedy, and a prosecution designed to silence him.

01 — THE STORYWhat Happened

I identified a foreign psychological warfare operator embedded within the Canadian Armed Forces who was targeting soldiers and military mental health support networks. I reported it through proper channels.

Instead of an investigation into the foreign threat, what followed was a campaign of retaliation against me and my family. A family member was killed. My sister, who was expecting a child, lost her partner — and subsequently lost her child.

Rather than pursuing justice for my family, military police investigators arrived at my home. I believe their intent was to provoke a confrontation. When that failed, they turned to the courts — using a prosecution that I believe is designed not to deliver justice, but to destroy my credibility and silence what I witnessed.

02 — TIMELINESequence of Events

  • Phase 1 Identified a foreign national operating a psychological warfare campaign within the Canadian Forces, targeting soldiers and mental health support infrastructure.
  • Phase 2 Reported findings through available channels. No meaningful investigation was initiated into the foreign interference.
  • Phase 3 A foreign national connected to the operation killed a member of my family. My pregnant sister lost her partner, and later lost her unborn child as a result.
  • Phase 4 Canadian Forces National Investigation Service (CFNIS) investigators arrived at my residence. I believe the encounter was designed to provoke a violent reaction.
  • Phase 5 A prosecution was initiated against me. I believe this prosecution is politically motivated — designed to use the legal system as a weapon to silence a witness to foreign interference and cover up failures within military police.

03 — THE PATTERNLawfare Against Whistleblowers

What happened to me follows a documented pattern: when individuals report inconvenient truths about institutional failures or foreign compromise, the institution retaliates — not against the threat, but against the person who reported it.

This is not about one person. This is about whether Canadians can trust that their military police and prosecutors serve the public interest, or whether these institutions can be weaponized against the citizens they are supposed to protect.

When the people who are supposed to protect you become the people you need protection from, the system is broken.

04 — WHAT I AM ASKING FORAccountability

I am asking for three things:

1. An independent investigation into the foreign interference I reported, conducted by an authority outside the Canadian Forces chain of command.

2. A review of my prosecution by an independent body to determine whether it meets the standard of legitimate prosecution or constitutes abuse of process.

3. Justice for my family. A family member was killed. A child was lost. These are not abstractions. These are human lives destroyed by people who believed they were above accountability.

STATEMENT OF CLAIM: SEVERE MILITARY NEGLIGENCE

1. NATURE OF THE ACTION: THE "EMPLOYED RETARD" CLAUSE
The Plaintiff formally alleges that the Princess Patricia's Canadian Light Infantry (PPCLI) and its associated Kit Shop committed an act of catastrophic, systemic negligence by knowingly employing a mentally challenged individual as a Combat Signaler in an active warzone.
2. FACTUAL BACKGROUND: THE COMMUNICATION COLLAPSE
Military operations rely absolutely on the secure, rapid, and accurate transmission of tactical intelligence. The role of a Signaler is zero-fail. The Plaintiff contends that the PPCLI deliberately deployed a "retard" incapable of basic human communication to man the radio nets during hostile engagements.
When artillery fire was required, the Defendant simply grunted into the handset. When MedEvac routing was paramount, the Defendant engaged in aggressive, non-verbal drooling. At no point did the PPCLI chain of command intervene, choosing instead to let a man who could not tie his own combat boots dictate battlefield cryptography.
3. THE KIT SHOP LIABILITY
The PPCLI Kit Shop is explicitly named in this suit for supplying the aforementioned impaired Signaler with high-grade tactical kit and shiny patches, falsely projecting an aura of military competence that disastrously misled allied forces into trusting him with a C9 light machine gun and a PRC-117 radio.
4. REMEDIES SOUGHT
The Plaintiff demands:
  • Immediate halting of all PPCLI military operations until they administer a second-grade reading comprehension test to their Signalers.
  • Financial compensation in the amount of $1,954 (the calculated value of a Canadian life under MAID) for the emotional damages suffered while listening to the Defendant hyperventilate on the command net.
  • A formal, written apology from the PPCLI Kit Shop for outfitting a tactical hazard.

Lobbying Revolving Door — Ministers and Senior Officials Become Lobbyists for Industries They Regulated
How it works: The Lobbying Act imposes a 5-year cooling-off period on former ministers lobbying their former departments. Documented examples: Former Health Minister Jane Philpott → public sector consulting. Former Finance officials → Bay Street. Former CRTC members → telecom industry. Pattern is consistent across all governing parties.

Finding: Commissioner of Lobbying annual reports document hundreds of registrations by former senior officials each year. The registry is public at lobbyists.gc.ca.
Lobbyist Registry — Live Commissioner Annual Reports

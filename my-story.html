6 Years of Lawfare
For Telling the Truth

I reported foreign interference inside the Canadian military. Instead of investigating the threat, they spent six years trying to destroy me.

📢 Share this. Every share is a witness.

← Back to main report

01 — WHAT I FOUNDForeign Interference Inside the Canadian Forces

I identified what I believe to be a foreign psychological warfare operation embedded within the Canadian Armed Forces. The operation was targeting Canadian soldiers and the mental health support networks designed to protect them.

This was not a minor security concern. This was a foreign state actor operating inside our military's most sensitive support infrastructure — the systems that soldiers depend on when they are at their most vulnerable.

I reported what I found through available channels. I expected an investigation. What I got instead was the full weight of the Canadian military justice system turned against me.

02 — WHAT HAPPENED NEXTMy Family Was Destroyed

After I reported what I found:

  • A foreign national connected to the operation killed a member of my family.
  • My sister, who was pregnant at the time, lost her partner — and subsequently lost her unborn child as a result of the trauma.
  • Rather than investigating the killing or the foreign interference I reported, the Canadian Forces National Investigation Service (CFNIS) targeted me.
  • A CFNIS investigator arrived at my home. I believe the encounter was designed to provoke a violent confrontation. I believe the intent was for me to react in a way that would justify the use of force against me.
  • When provocation failed, a prosecution was initiated against me — one that has now continued for six years.

03 — SIX YEARSThe Prosecution That Will Not End

For six years, I have been subjected to a prosecution that I believe is not about justice. I believe it is about silencing a witness.

Consider what six years of prosecution does to a person:

  • Financial destruction — Legal defence is not free. Six years of court appearances, legal fees, and the inability to move forward with your life.
  • Psychological warfare — The process itself is the punishment. Every court date, every delay, every procedural motion is designed to grind you down until you give up or break.
  • Social isolation — When the state accuses you, people distance themselves. Your reputation is destroyed before you ever see a courtroom.
  • Employment destruction — Try maintaining a career while the government prosecutes you for years. The charge alone is enough to end opportunities.

This is not justice. This is lawfare — the weaponization of legal systems against individuals the state wants to silence. It is a recognized tactic documented by human rights organizations worldwide.

04 — THE PATTERNThis Is Not Unique to Me

Canada has a documented history of retaliating against whistleblowers rather than investigating what they report. My case follows a pattern:

  • Vice-Admiral Mark Norman — Accused of leaking cabinet secrets. Charges stayed after years of prosecution. The government admitted it could not prove its case, but the damage to his career was already done.
  • Military sexual misconduct whistleblowers — Multiple service members who reported sexual assault within the CAF faced career retaliation rather than seeing their reports investigated.
  • Intelligence community whistleblowers — Canada has no dedicated whistleblower protection legislation comparable to the U.S. Whistleblower Protection Act. Those who report misconduct in national security contexts have effectively zero legal protection.

The message is clear: if you report something the institution doesn't want reported, the institution will destroy you.

05 — THE BIGGER PICTUREForeign Interference in Canada Is Real

The Government of Canada itself has acknowledged that foreign interference is a serious and ongoing threat. In 2023:

  • The Public Inquiry into Foreign Interference (Hogue Commission) was established to investigate foreign interference in Canadian democratic processes.
  • CSIS Director David Vigneault confirmed that foreign states, including the People's Republic of China, have been conducting interference operations on Canadian soil.
  • The NSICOP Special Report (2024) identified parliamentarians who had, wittingly or unwittingly, assisted foreign state actors.
  • The Government of Canada created the Foreign Interference Commission and passed the Countering Foreign Interference Act (Bill C-70, 2024).

The government acknowledges the threat exists. The government created commissions, passed legislation, and held public inquiries. And yet, when I reported foreign interference inside the military itself, the response was not investigation — it was prosecution.

If Canada is serious about countering foreign interference, why is it prosecuting the people who report it?

06 — THE COSTWhat Happens When Whistleblowers Are Silenced

When institutions silence the people who report threats, the threats grow unchecked. The consequences are not theoretical. They are measurable in Canadian deaths:

  • 76,475 Canadians have been killed through MAID since 2016 — verified from Health Canada's own annual reports.
  • 149 people killed by MAID in 2024 alone who could have received disability support but didn't. Health Canada published this number.[HC 6th Report]
  • 47% of Track 2 MAID recipients reported loneliness and isolation as a source of suffering. 49% felt like a burden on others.
  • Canadian veterans die by suicide at 1.4× (male) and 1.9× (female) the civilian rate — Veterans Affairs Canada's own research.
  • A Paralympian veteran asked for a wheelchair ramp. Veterans Affairs offered her death. At least 4–5 such cases were referred to the RCMP.
  • The disability benefits backlog doubled to 11,000+ in a single year. Only 47% of applications meet the 16-week standard.

The foreign interference I reported was targeting the mental health infrastructure that serves soldiers. The same soldiers who, when they leave service, face a system that denies their disability claims, makes them wait months for help, and offers them death when they ask for a wheelchair ramp.

Silencing me didn't make the threat go away. It made it worse. Every day that the foreign interference I reported goes uninvestigated is a day that Canadian soldiers remain exposed to a compromised support system.

They spent 6 years prosecuting the person who reported the threat. They spent 0 days investigating the threat itself. Ask yourself who benefits from that decision — and who dies because of it.

07 — THE SYSTEMHow the Machine Works

Here is the system as I experienced it, laid out in sequence:

Step 1: Soldier identifies threat to Canadian Forces → Reports it

Step 2: CFNIS receives report → Does NOT investigate the threat

Step 3: CFNIS investigates the REPORTER instead

Step 4: Prosecution initiated → Whistleblower's credibility destroyed

Step 5: Foreign interference continues unchecked

Step 6: Soldiers remain exposed → Veterans denied support → MAID offered

Result: The threat grows. The witness is silenced. Canadians die.

This is not a conspiracy theory. Every step above can be verified:

  • Step 1–4: My lived experience, documented in court records spanning 6 years.
  • Step 5: The Hogue Commission, NSICOP reports, and CSIS Director testimony all confirm ongoing foreign interference that Canada has failed to counter.
  • Step 6: Veterans Affairs' own data confirms the suicide rates, benefit denials, and MAID being offered instead of support.

The question every Canadian should ask: if the system protects the threat and punishes the person who reports it, whose interests does that system serve?

08 — WHAT I AM ASKING FORThree Things

1. An independent investigation into the foreign interference I reported, conducted by an authority with no connection to the Canadian Forces chain of command or the CFNIS.

2. An independent review of my prosecution to determine whether it constitutes a legitimate exercise of prosecutorial discretion or an abuse of process designed to silence a witness.

3. Justice for my family. A family member was killed. An unborn child was lost. Six years later, there has been no investigation, no accountability, and no justice. My family deserves better. Canada deserves better.

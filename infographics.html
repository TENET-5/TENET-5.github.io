International Law Violations: A Side-by-Side

Legal principles established at Nuremberg (1947), Geneva (1949), and Rome (1998) — and how Canada's MAID program intersects with each

Legal Principle What It Says What Canada Did
Nuremberg Code, Principle 1 (1947) Voluntary consent is absolutely essential. The person must have sufficient understanding to make an enlightened decision. 149 people killed in 2024 could have received disability support but didn't. 47% of Track 2 recipients cited loneliness. Is consent given under despair truly voluntary?
ICCPR Article 6(1) Every human being has the inherent right to life. No one shall be arbitrarily deprived of life. 76,475 state-administered deaths since 2016. The poor and disabled are killed at 2.42× the expected rate.
Rome Statute, Article 7(1)(a) Murder, when committed as part of a widespread or systematic attack against a civilian population. 16,499 deaths in a single year (2024). Systematic: written into federal law. Widespread: 5.05% of all deaths. Directed against civilians: disproportionately the vulnerable.
Rome Statute, Article 7(2)(a) "Attack directed against any civilian population" means a course of conduct pursuant to a State or organizational policy. MAID is federal law (Bill C-7, Bill C-14). It is government policy. The killing is conducted by state-sanctioned medical professionals.
CRPD Article 10 States shall ensure the right to life of persons with disabilities on an equal basis. Track 2 MAID recipients from the most vulnerable disability quintile: 56.9% (expected: 20%). A 2.85× overrepresentation.
Geneva Convention Common Article 3 Prohibits violence to life and person, in particular murder of all kinds, of persons taking no active part in hostilities. Veterans offered MAID instead of wheelchair ramps. At least 4-5 cases referred to RCMP. Military personnel targeted by their own support system.

Sources: Nuremberg Code [19], Rome Statute [12], ICCPR Art. 6, CRPD Art. 10, Geneva Conventions Common Art. 3

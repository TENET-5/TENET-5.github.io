1940s

Post-War Reconstruction
1945 MKMackenzie King Liberal Veterans
Veterans Charter
Comprehensive benefits package for returning WWII soldiers: housing assistance, education funding, land grants, and rehabilitation programs. One of Canada's most successful social programs ever enacted.
💀💀💀💀💀Low harm — positive action
1947 MKMackenzie King Liberal Immigration
Canadian Citizenship Act
Canada creates its own citizenship for the first time. Previously, Canadians were British subjects. The Act established the legal concept of Canadian citizenship independent of Britain.
💀💀💀💀💀Low harm — foundational legislation
1949 LSLouis St. Laurent Liberal Foreign Policy
NATO Founding Member
Canada becomes one of 12 founding members of the North Atlantic Treaty Organization. Commits to collective defence in the Cold War era. Newfoundland also joins Confederation this year.
💀💀💀💀💀Low harm — alliance formation

1950s

Cold War & Broken Promises
1950–53 LSLouis St. Laurent Liberal Military
Korean War
Canada commits over 26,000 troops to the UN force in Korea. 516 Canadians killed in action, thousands more wounded. Veterans later described feeling forgotten by their own government upon return.
💀💀💀💀💀Moderate — 516 killed, minimal post-war support
1957 JDJohn Diefenbaker Conservative Military
NORAD Established, Avro Arrow Program Active
NORAD (North American Aerospace Defense Command) created as a joint Canada-US defence agreement. The CF-105 Avro Arrow interceptor program is advancing — the most advanced fighter jet of its era.
💀💀💀💀💀Low harm — defence modernization
1959 JDJohn Diefenbaker Conservative Economic
Avro Arrow Cancelled — 14,000 Jobs Destroyed
Diefenbaker cancels the CF-105 Arrow program and orders all prototypes, tooling, and blueprints destroyed. 14,000 Avro Canada employees laid off. Top engineers flee to NASA and US defence firms. Canada's indigenous aerospace industry never recovers.
💀💀💀💀💀Catastrophic — permanently destroyed Canadian aerospace

1960s

Rights & Revolution
1960 JDJohn Diefenbaker Conservative Indigenous
Indigenous People Granted Federal Voting Rights
Status Indians given the right to vote in federal elections without losing their treaty rights. Previously, Indigenous people had to renounce their status to vote — a deliberate exclusion since Confederation.
💀💀💀💀💀Low harm — overdue correction
1967 LPLester Pearson Liberal Immigration
Points-Based Immigration System Introduced
Canada replaces race-based immigration preferences with a points system evaluating skills, education, and language ability. First of its kind globally. Eliminates formal racial discrimination in immigration policy.
💀💀💀💀💀Low harm — progressive reform
1968–69 PTPierre Trudeau Liberal Constitutional
Pierre Trudeau Elected; Official Languages Act
Pierre Trudeau wins election on Trudeaumania wave. Passes the Official Languages Act (1969), making English and French co-equal official languages of the federal government. Transforms federal public service requirements.
💀💀💀💀💀Moderate — divisive but constitutional
1969 PTPierre Trudeau Liberal Indigenous
White Paper on Indian Policy
Jean Chrétien (then Indian Affairs Minister) proposes abolishing the Indian Act and all treaty rights, framing assimilation as “equality.” Rejected forcefully by First Nations leaders. Galvanizes the modern Indigenous rights movement.
💀💀💀💀💀High harm — attempted forced assimilation

1970s

Crisis & Multiculturalism
1970 PTPierre Trudeau Liberal Constitutional
October Crisis — War Measures Act Invoked
After FLQ kidnappings of James Cross and Pierre Laporte (murdered), Trudeau invokes the War Measures Act — the only peacetime use in Canadian history. Nearly 500 people arrested without charge in Quebec. Civil liberties suspended.
💀💀💀💀💀High harm — mass arrests, civil liberties suspended
1971 PTPierre Trudeau Liberal Social
Official Multiculturalism Policy
Canada becomes the first country to adopt multiculturalism as official government policy, affirming cultural diversity within a bilingual framework. Later enshrined in the 1988 Multiculturalism Act.
💀💀💀💀💀Low harm — policy framework
1974 PTPierre Trudeau Liberal Economic
Foreign Investment Review Agency (FIRA)
FIRA established to screen foreign takeovers of Canadian businesses. Aimed at preserving Canadian economic sovereignty. Later dismantled by Mulroney and replaced with Investment Canada (pro-foreign-investment).
💀💀💀💀💀Moderate — economic intervention
1976 RLRené Lévesque (QC) PQ Constitutional
Parti Québécois Elected in Quebec
René Lévesque's PQ wins a majority in Quebec on a platform of sovereignty-association. Begins the modern separatist era. Passes Bill 101 (Charter of the French Language) in 1977, reshaping Quebec's linguistic landscape.
💀💀💀💀💀Moderate — national unity crisis begins

1980s

Constitution & Free Trade
1980 PTPierre Trudeau Liberal Economic
National Energy Program (NEP)
Federal government seizes control of oil pricing and revenues from Alberta. Devastates the Alberta economy, triggers capital flight, and creates lasting Western alienation against Ottawa. Estimated cost to Alberta: $50–100 billion.
💀💀💀💀💀Catastrophic — devastated Alberta, permanent political damage
1980 PTPierre Trudeau Liberal Constitutional
Quebec Referendum — 60% No
First Quebec sovereignty referendum. 59.56% vote No. Trudeau promises constitutional renewal in exchange for the No vote — a promise many Quebecers later feel was betrayed by the 1982 patriation without Quebec's consent.
💀💀💀💀💀Moderate — national unity preserved but promises broken
1982 PTPierre Trudeau Liberal Constitutional
Constitution Patriated with Charter of Rights — Quebec Excluded
Constitution repatriated from Britain with the new Charter of Rights and Freedoms. Negotiated via the “Kitchen Accord” — Quebec was excluded from the final deal. Quebec has never formally signed the Constitution. A wound that persists to this day.
💀💀💀💀💀High harm — Quebec excluded, national unity fractured
1984 BMBrian Mulroney Conservative Constitutional
Mulroney Elected — Largest Majority in History
Brian Mulroney wins 211 of 282 seats, the largest majority government in Canadian history. Promises to bring Quebec into the constitutional family and reduce the deficit. Sets stage for Meech Lake and free trade.
💀💀💀💀💀Low harm — democratic mandate
1987–90 BMBrian Mulroney Conservative Constitutional
Meech Lake Accord — Fails
Attempt to bring Quebec into the Constitution with “distinct society” clause. Fails in 1990 when Manitoba (Elijah Harper) and Newfoundland do not ratify before the deadline. Deepens Quebec alienation and fuels separatism.
💀💀💀💀💀High harm — constitutional failure, separatism surges
1988 BMBrian Mulroney Conservative Economic
Canada-US Free Trade Agreement
Landmark trade agreement eliminates most tariffs between Canada and the US. Wins the “free trade election” of 1988. Critics warn of manufacturing job losses and economic dependence. Precursor to NAFTA.
💀💀💀💀💀Moderate — trade boom but manufacturing erosion
1989–91 BMBrian Mulroney Conservative Economic
GST Introduced
Goods and Services Tax (7%, later 5%) replaces the hidden 13.5% Manufacturers' Sales Tax. Massively unpopular but more transparent and economically efficient. Chrétien promises to scrap it — never does.
💀💀💀💀💀Moderate — regressive tax, but replaced worse system

1990s

Scandals & Near-Secession
1992 BMBrian Mulroney Conservative Constitutional
Charlottetown Accord Rejected by Referendum
Second attempt at constitutional reform. Put to national referendum and rejected by 54% of voters. Both Quebec nationalists and Western populists vote No (for opposite reasons). Constitutional reform declared dead.
💀💀💀💀💀Moderate — constitutional reform dies
1993 BMMulroney / Campbell Conservative Scandal
Somalia Affair — Airborne Regiment Disbanded
Canadian Airborne Regiment members torture and kill Shidane Arone, a Somali teenager. Military leadership covers up evidence, destroys documents. Regiment disbanded in 1995. Commission of inquiry shut down by government before completion.
💀💀💀💀💀Catastrophic — murder, cover-up, institutional betrayal
1993–95 JCJean Chrétien Liberal Economic
Chrétien Elected; NAFTA Implemented; Gun Registry Begins
Chrétien wins massive majority. NAFTA takes effect Jan 1, 1994. Long-gun registry launched with $2M budget — eventual cost exceeds $629M+ before being scrapped by Harper in 2012. Becomes a symbol of government waste and incompetence.
💀💀💀💀💀High harm — $629M+ wasted on ineffective registry
1995 JCJean Chrétien Liberal Constitutional
Quebec Referendum — 50.58% No (Closest Call)
Canada nearly breaks apart. 50.58% No, 49.42% Yes on Quebec sovereignty. Margin of ~54,000 votes. Chrétien government accused of complacency until the final weeks. Parizeau blames “money and the ethnic vote” in concession speech.
💀💀💀💀💀Catastrophic — country nearly dissolved
1996 JCJean Chrétien Liberal Scandal
Sponsorship Program Begins (Later: AdScam)
Federal government launches a $332M sponsorship program to promote national unity in Quebec. Millions funnelled to Liberal-connected advertising firms for little or no work. Full scandal exposed by Auditor General in 2004.
💀💀💀💀💀High harm — massive public corruption
1997 JCJean Chrétien Liberal Indigenous
Last Residential School Closes
The last federally-run residential school (Gordon Residential School, Saskatchewan) closes its doors. The system operated for over 130 years, forcibly removing ~150,000 Indigenous children. Thousands died. Cultural genocide officially ends — but the damage is generational.
💀💀💀💀💀Catastrophic — 130+ years of cultural genocide ends
1998–2000 JCJean Chrétien Liberal Constitutional
Clarity Act Process
Following the 1998 Supreme Court Reference on Secession, Chrétien passes the Clarity Act (2000), requiring a “clear majority” on a “clear question” for any future secession vote. Quebec responds with its own Bill 99 asserting self-determination.
💀💀💀💀💀Moderate — legal framework, politically divisive

2000s

War, Scandal & Reckoning
2001–14 JCChrétien / Martin / Harper Liberal Military
Afghanistan Mission — 158 Killed, 2,000+ Wounded
Canada deploys to Afghanistan after 9/11. Over 40,000 CAF members serve across 13 years. 158 killed, over 2,000 physically wounded, thousands more with PTSD. Veterans return to inadequate support, closed VAC offices, and a system that fights their claims.
💀💀💀💀💀High harm — 158 dead, thousands with PTSD, poor veteran care
2002 JCJean Chrétien Liberal Social
Romanow Report on Healthcare
Roy Romanow's Commission on the Future of Health Care recommends major federal reinvestment in public healthcare. Many recommendations ignored. Two decades later, the healthcare system is in crisis with record wait times and staff shortages.
💀💀💀💀💀Moderate — warnings ignored, crisis decades later
2003 JCJean Chrétien Liberal Foreign Policy
Canada Declines Iraq War Participation
Chrétien refuses to join the US-led invasion of Iraq without UN authorization. One of the most consequential foreign policy decisions in modern Canadian history. Widely praised in hindsight given the absence of WMDs.
💀💀💀💀💀Low harm — sound judgment
2004 PMPaul Martin Liberal Scandal
Sponsorship Scandal Exposed by Auditor General
AG Sheila Fraser reveals $100M+ in sponsorship funds went to Liberal-connected firms for little or no work. Gomery Commission follows. Several officials convicted. Destroys Liberal credibility in Quebec for a generation.
💀💀💀💀💀Catastrophic — massive corruption, public trust shattered
2005 PMPaul Martin Liberal Indigenous
Kelowna Accord ($5B for Indigenous) — Never Implemented
Historic $5-billion agreement between federal, provincial, territorial, and Indigenous leaders to close gaps in education, health, housing, and economic opportunity. Harper government cancels it after winning 2006 election. A generation of progress lost.
💀💀💀💀💀Catastrophic — best chance at reconciliation, cancelled
2005 PMPaul Martin Liberal Social
Same-Sex Marriage Legalized (Civil Marriage Act)
Canada becomes the fourth country globally and first in the Americas to legalize same-sex marriage nationwide through the Civil Marriage Act (Bill C-38). Upheld by courts as consistent with the Charter.
💀💀💀💀💀Low harm — rights expansion
2006 SHStephen Harper Conservative Constitutional
Harper Elected; Federal Accountability Act
Harper wins minority on anti-corruption platform. Passes the Federal Accountability Act — tightening lobbying rules, banning corporate/union donations, strengthening the Auditor General and Ethics Commissioner. Also apologizes for the Chinese head tax.
💀💀💀💀💀Low harm — accountability reform
2007–08 SHStephen Harper Conservative Indigenous
Residential Schools Settlement & Formal Apology
Indian Residential Schools Settlement Agreement (2007) — largest class-action settlement in Canadian history. Harper delivers formal apology in Parliament (June 11, 2008). Establishes Truth and Reconciliation Commission. A significant but late first step.
💀💀💀💀💀Moderate — apology given, but action slow
2008–09 SHStephen Harper Conservative Scandal
Harper Prorogues Parliament Twice
Dec 2008: Prorogues to avoid a confidence vote from the Liberal-NDP coalition. Dec 2009: Prorogues again to avoid releasing Afghan detainee transfer documents. Sets precedent for using prorogation as a political escape hatch.
💀💀💀💀💀Moderate — abuse of parliamentary procedure

2010s

Pipeline Politics & MAID
2011 SHStephen Harper Conservative Military
Canada Withdraws from Afghanistan Combat
Combat mission ends in Kandahar. Transitions to training role in Kabul until 2014. Canada's longest war concludes with 158 dead, 2,000+ wounded, and a veteran community that will fight for recognition and support for years to come.
💀💀💀💀💀Moderate — war ends, veterans abandoned
2012–15 SHStephen Harper Conservative Scandal
Senate Expense Scandal
Senators Mike Duffy, Pamela Wallin, and Patrick Brazeau exposed for improper expense claims. Harper's chief of staff Nigel Wright secretly pays Duffy's $90,000 tab. Duffy charged but acquitted; judge blames the system. Reinforces public contempt for the Senate.
💀💀💀💀💀Moderate — institutional corruption
2014 SHStephen Harper Conservative Social
Bill C-36 (Prostitution) & Parliament Hill Shootings
Bill C-36 criminalizes purchasing sex (Nordic model). In October, Michael Zehaf-Bibeau shoots Cpl. Nathan Cirillo at the National War Memorial and storms Parliament before being killed. Leads to expanded security and anti-terror legislation.
💀💀💀💀💀Moderate — terror attack, legislative response
2015 SHStephen Harper Conservative Indigenous
Truth and Reconciliation Commission Final Report
TRC releases final report with 94 Calls to Action addressing the legacy of residential schools. Calls the system “cultural genocide.” As of 2024, fewer than 15 of the 94 Calls have been fully implemented by any level of government.
💀💀💀💀💀High harm — cultural genocide documented, action stalled
2015 JTJustin Trudeau Liberal Constitutional
Justin Trudeau Elected (Majority)
Trudeau wins majority on promises of electoral reform, Indigenous reconciliation, transparency, and balanced budgets by 2019. Electoral reform abandoned within 18 months. Balanced budgets never achieved. Begins decade of record spending and debt.
💀💀💀💀💀Moderate — broken promises set tone for decade
2016 JTJustin Trudeau Liberal Social
MAID Legalized (Bill C-14)
Medical Assistance in Dying legalized following the Carter v. Canada Supreme Court ruling. Initially limited to those whose death is “reasonably foreseeable.” Later expanded dramatically, raising concerns about vulnerable populations choosing death over inadequate social support.
💀💀💀💀💀Moderate — rights vs. safeguard concerns
2016 JTJustin Trudeau Liberal Scandal
Phoenix Pay System Launched — $5B+ Disaster
IBM-built Phoenix pay system launched to pay 300,000+ federal employees. Immediately fails catastrophically — workers go months without pay, receive incorrect amounts, or are overpaid and told to return money. Cost exceeds $5 billion with no end in sight.
💀💀💀💀💀Catastrophic — $5B+ wasted, 300,000 workers affected
2016 JTJustin Trudeau Liberal Economic
Carbon Tax Announced
Federal carbon pricing announced at $10/tonne, rising annually. Provinces that don't implement their own system get the federal backstop. Becomes one of the most divisive economic policies of the era, challenged by multiple provinces in court.
💀💀💀💀💀Moderate — economic impact, political division
2017 JTJustin Trudeau Liberal Social
Bill C-16 (Gender Identity) & Feminist Foreign Policy
Bill C-16 adds gender identity and expression to the Canadian Human Rights Act and Criminal Code as protected grounds. Also announces the Feminist International Assistance Policy, redirecting foreign aid through a gender lens.
💀💀💀💀💀Moderate — divisive social policy
2018 JTJustin Trudeau Liberal Economic
Trans Mountain Pipeline Purchased — $4.5B → $34.2B
Government buys Trans Mountain pipeline from Kinder Morgan for $4.5B after regulatory delays threaten the project. Expansion costs balloon to $34.2B total. A self-proclaimed climate government nationalizing a pipeline — satisfying no one.
💀💀💀💀💀Catastrophic — $34.2B, ideological contradiction
2018 JTJustin Trudeau Liberal Social
Cannabis Legalized (Bill C-45)
Canada becomes the second country globally to legalize recreational cannabis. Bill C-45 (Cannabis Act) creates a regulated market. Pardons for prior possession convictions follow slowly. Black market persists due to high taxation and regulation.
💀💀💀💀💀Low harm — legalization reform
2019 JTJustin Trudeau Liberal Scandal
SNC-Lavalin Affair — Ethics Commissioner Finding
PM's office pressures AG Jody Wilson-Raybould to offer SNC-Lavalin a deferred prosecution agreement. She refuses and is removed from cabinet. Ethics Commissioner finds Trudeau violated the Conflict of Interest Act. Two cabinet ministers resign in protest.
💀💀💀💀💀High harm — obstruction of justice, ethical violation
2019 JTJustin Trudeau Liberal Foreign Policy
NSICOP Report on Foreign Interference (Classified)
National Security and Intelligence Committee of Parliamentarians issues classified report warning of foreign interference in Canadian democratic institutions. Government sits on it. Full public reckoning doesn't come until 2023 media leaks.
💀💀💀💀💀High harm — foreign interference suppressed

2020s

Emergencies, Debt & Collapse
2020 JTJustin Trudeau Liberal Economic
COVID Lockdowns — CERB/CEWS ($89.9B per Fraser Institute)
Pandemic spending totals hundreds of billions. CERB ($2,000/month), CEWS wage subsidies, and other programs. Fraser Institute estimates $89.9B went to recipients who didn't need it or didn't qualify. National debt explodes. Inflation follows.
💀💀💀💀💀Catastrophic — $89.9B wasted, debt explosion, inflation
2020 JTJustin Trudeau Liberal Scandal
WE Charity Scandal — Parliament Prorogued
Government awards $912M student volunteer program to WE Charity, which paid Trudeau family members ~$500K in speaking fees. Ethics Commissioner investigates. Trudeau prorogues Parliament to shut down committee investigations. Third ethics violation.
💀💀💀💀💀High harm — conflict of interest, prorogation abuse
2020 JTJustin Trudeau Liberal Scandal
Nova Scotia Mass Shooting (22 Dead) — RCMP Lucki Interference
Canada's deadliest mass shooting: 22 killed in Nova Scotia. Mass Casualty Commission later reveals RCMP Commissioner Lucki pressured Nova Scotia RCMP to release firearm details to support the government's pending gun ban. Political interference in a massacre investigation.
💀💀💀💀💀Catastrophic — 22 dead, political exploitation
2020 JTJustin Trudeau Liberal Social
Order in Council Gun Ban (No Parliamentary Debate)
Government bans ~1,500 firearm models by Order in Council — bypassing Parliament entirely. Announced days after the Nova Scotia shooting. Targets legal gun owners, not the illegal firearms used in the shooting. Buyback program still not implemented years later.
💀💀💀💀💀High harm — bypassed Parliament, targets legal owners
2021 JTJustin Trudeau Liberal Indigenous
Residential School GPR Announcements
Ground-penetrating radar surveys at former residential school sites reveal potential unmarked burials, beginning with Kamloops (215 anomalies). National reckoning follows. SNC-Lavalin connected to some site surveys. Excavation and identification remain incomplete years later.
💀💀💀💀💀Catastrophic — scale of historical atrocity revealed
2022 JTJustin Trudeau Liberal Constitutional
Emergencies Act Invoked (Freedom Convoy) — Ruled Unconstitutional
First-ever invocation of the Emergencies Act to clear Freedom Convoy protests in Ottawa. Bank accounts frozen without court orders. Federal Court later rules the invocation was unreasonable and unconstitutional. Government appeals.
💀💀💀💀💀Catastrophic — ruled unconstitutional, bank accounts frozen
2022 JTJustin Trudeau Liberal Scandal
ArriveCAN Scandal — $59.5M
Border travel app ArriveCAN costs $59.5M — for an app estimated to be worth $80K–$250K. Auditor General finds rampant mismanagement, fake subcontractors, and potential fraud. RCMP investigation follows. GC Strategies principals charged.
💀💀💀💀💀Catastrophic — $59.5M for an app, fraud charges
2022 JTJustin Trudeau Liberal Social
MAID Expanded — Mental Illness Inclusion Planned
Bill C-7 expands MAID to those whose death is not reasonably foreseeable (Track 2). Mental illness as sole condition planned for 2024, then delayed to 2027. International concern that Canada is offering death to those denied adequate social support, housing, and healthcare.
💀💀💀💀💀High harm — vulnerable populations at risk
2023 JTJustin Trudeau Liberal Foreign Policy
Foreign Interference Inquiry (Hogue Commission)
After media leaks of CSIS intelligence, public inquiry into foreign interference led by Justice Marie-Josée Hogue begins. Examines interference by China, India, and others in Canadian elections and institutions. NSICOP later names sitting MPs connected to foreign states.
💀💀💀💀💀Catastrophic — democratic sovereignty compromised
2023 JTJustin Trudeau Liberal Scandal
Green Slush Fund (SDTC) Suspended
Sustainable Development Technology Canada (SDTC) suspended after whistleblowers reveal board members directing $390M+ to companies they had financial stakes in. AG confirms massive conflict of interest. Ethics and accountability structures failed completely.
💀💀💀💀💀High harm — $390M+ in conflicts of interest
2024 JTJustin Trudeau Liberal Social
Jasper Wildfire Destroys 358 Structures
Wildfire devastates the town of Jasper inside Jasper National Park, destroying 358 structures including homes and businesses. Federal land, federal responsibility. Questions raised about decades of inadequate forest management and fire mitigation on Parks Canada lands.
💀💀💀💀💀High harm — 358 structures, federal negligence
2024 JTJustin Trudeau Liberal Foreign Policy
NSICOP Names MPs Connected to Foreign States
National Security and Intelligence Committee of Parliamentarians releases report identifying sitting Members of Parliament who have been witting or semi-witting participants in foreign interference. Names remain classified, sparking public outrage and demands for transparency.
💀💀💀💀💀Catastrophic — elected officials serving foreign states
2025 MCMark Carney Liberal Constitutional
Trudeau Resigns; Carney Becomes PM (Never Elected)
Justin Trudeau resigns as Liberal leader. Mark Carney — former Bank of Canada and Bank of England governor — becomes Prime Minister without ever having held elected office. Sets carbon tax to 0% amid cost-of-living crisis. Faces Trump tariffs and economic turmoil.
💀💀💀💀💀High harm — unelected PM, economic crisis
2025 MCMark Carney Liberal Constitutional
Carney Wins Election (169 Seats); Ethics Investigation Begins
Carney calls snap election and wins 169 seats on anti-Trump nationalism. Ethics Commissioner launches investigation almost immediately. Governs with a mandate built on crisis rather than policy. Inherits record debt, housing crisis, and crumbling institutions.
💀💀💀💀💀Moderate — democratic mandate, ethics cloud
2025 MCMark Carney Liberal Scandal
Toronto Police Project South Corruption Probe
Project South investigation exposes corruption involving 7 Toronto police officers. Part of a broader pattern of institutional accountability failures across Canadian law enforcement. Follows years of RCMP scandals and eroding public trust in policing.
💀💀💀💀💀Moderate — institutional corruption

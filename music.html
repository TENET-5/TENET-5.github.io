Music That Fights Back

Every resistance needs a soundtrack. These bands have been mocking corrupt governments since before most politicians learned to lie.

Dead Kennedys

San Francisco hardcore punk. Jello Biafra ran for mayor. Their name alone triggers politicians.

Fresh Fruit for Rotting Vegetables

1980 — California Uber Alles. Holiday in Cambodia. Kill the Poor. The album that started it all.

Plastic Surgery Disasters

1982 — Government Flu. Terminal Preppie. The bleeding edge of political punk.

Frankenchrist

1985 — The album that got Jello Biafra arrested for obscenity. The state tried to silence punk. It failed.

Bedtime for Democracy

1986 — Their final album. Rambozo the Clown. Anarchy for Sale. Still relevant 40 years later.

Propagandhi

Winnipeg skate-thrash. The most politically literate band in punk. They cite sources in their liner notes.

How to Clean Everything

1993 — Skate punk meets anarchist political theory. Anti-Manifesto. Stick the Fucking Flag Up Your Goddamn Ass You Sonofabitch.

Today's Empires, Tomorrow's Ashes

2001 — The title says everything. Purina Hall of Fame. Back to the Motor League.

Supporting Caste

2009 — Night Letters. Dear Coaches Corner. Human(e) Meat. Technical thrash with footnotes.

Victory Lap

2017 — Their best album. When All Your Fears Collide. Comply/Resist. Adventures in Zoochosis.

Why This Music Matters

Punk didn't just make noise — it documented the abuses that mainstream media wouldn't touch. Dead Kennedys wrote about police brutality and government overreach in 1980. D.O.A. named an entire genre while fighting fascism from Vancouver. Propagandhi cited academic sources in their liner notes while shredding faster than anyone in Winnipeg.

When the government prosecutes whistleblowers, expands state-assisted death to the disabled, and ignores foreign interference in its own elections — this is the music that says what Parliament won't. Turn it up.

Music That Fights Back

Every resistance needs a soundtrack. These bands have been mocking corrupt governments since before most politicians learned to lie.

Dead Kennedys

San Francisco hardcore punk. Jello Biafra ran for mayor. Their name alone triggers politicians.

Fresh Fruit for Rotting Vegetables

1980 — California Uber Alles. Holiday in Cambodia. Kill the Poor. The album that started it all.

Plastic Surgery Disasters

1982 — Government Flu. Terminal Preppie. The bleeding edge of political punk.

Frankenchrist

1985 — The album that got Jello Biafra arrested for obscenity. The state tried to silence punk. It failed.

Bedtime for Democracy

1986 — Their final album. Rambozo the Clown. Anarchy for Sale. Still relevant 40 years later.

Why This Music Matters

Punk didn't just make noise — it documented the abuses that mainstream media wouldn't touch. Dead Kennedys wrote about police brutality and government overreach in 1980. Jello Biafra ran for mayor of San Francisco. The state tried to prosecute him for obscenity. He won.

When the government prosecutes whistleblowers, expands state-assisted death to the disabled, and ignores foreign interference in its own elections — this is the music that says what Parliament won't. Turn it up.

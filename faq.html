Answering the Objections

Every defence of Canada's MAID program can be answered with the government's own data. Here are the most common objections and why they fail.

← Back to main report

OBJECTION 1"MAID is voluntary. People choose it freely."

The government's own data contradicts this.

  • 47% of Track 2 recipients cited loneliness and isolation as a source of suffering.
  • 49% felt like a burden on family, friends, or caregivers.
  • 48.3% came from the poorest housing quintile (expected: 20%) — a 2.42× overrepresentation.
  • 149 people killed in 2024 could have received disability support services but did not.

When nearly half the people choosing death cite loneliness, burden, and poverty — and nearly half come from the most disadvantaged neighbourhoods — that is not free choice. That is a system offering death to people it has failed to support.

The Nuremberg Code requires consent to be free of "any element of force, fraud, deceit, duress, overreaching, or other ulterior form of constraint or coercion." Poverty, isolation, and institutional abandonment are coercion.

Sources: Health Canada 6th Annual Report (2024), Ontario Coroner data, Nuremberg Code (1947)

OBJECTION 2"Most MAID recipients have terminal cancer. This is about dignity in dying."

True for Track 1. Irrelevant for Track 2.

Yes, 63% of MAID recipients in 2024 had cancer as their primary condition, and 95.6% were on Track 1 (death reasonably foreseeable). For those patients, the dignity argument has merit.

But 732 people in 2024 were on Track 2 — their death was NOT reasonably foreseeable. These are people with chronic conditions, disabilities, and mental suffering who are not dying. The system is killing them because it has not provided adequate alternatives.

And Track 2 is growing. In March 2027, mental illness alone will qualify. Depression. PTSD. Anxiety. The same conditions affecting veterans who already die by suicide at 1.4–1.9× the civilian rate.

Source: Health Canada 6th Annual Report (2024)

OBJECTION 3"Canada's MAID program has safeguards."

The safeguards are not working.

  • A Paralympian veteran asked for a wheelchair ramp. She was offered death. This passed through all "safeguards."
  • At least 4–5 veterans were offered MAID by a VAC employee. This passed through all "safeguards."
  • 149 people who could have received disability support were killed instead. The "safeguards" did not catch this.
  • Track 2 recipients from the poorest quintile are overrepresented at 2.42×. The "safeguards" did not flag this pattern.

A safeguard that does not prevent the killing of people who could have been helped is not a safeguard. It is a rubber stamp.

Sources: Parliamentary testimony (2022), Health Canada 6th Annual Report (2024)

OBJECTION 4"The growth rate is slowing down. The program is stabilizing."

Slowing from 36.8% to 7.5% annual growth is still exponential growth.

At 7.5% annual growth (the government's lowest recorded rate):

2024: 16,499 → 2026: 19,066 → 2028: 22,033 → 2030: 25,462

Cumulative by 2030: 204,954 Canadians killed

"Stabilizing" at 45 people per day is not a defence. It is an admission that killing 45 Canadians daily has become routine.

And this projection does NOT include mental illness eligibility (March 2027), which every expert expects to significantly increase numbers.

Source: Health Canada annual reports, mathematical projection using government's own growth rate

OBJECTION 5"Other countries have assisted dying too."

Canada kills at a rate matched only by the Netherlands.

  • Netherlands: ~5.4% of all deaths
  • Canada: 5.1% of all deaths (2024)
  • Belgium: ~3.6% of all deaths
  • Australia, Spain, New Zealand: all below 1%

Canada now has the second-highest rate of state-administered death in the world, behind only the Netherlands. But Canada's rate is growing faster — the Netherlands has plateaued while Canada's CAGR is 41.6%. At current trajectory, Canada will overtake the Netherlands within 1–2 years.

More importantly: no other country has expanded eligibility to include people whose death is NOT foreseeable at the rate Canada has, and no other country is preparing to include mental illness as a sole qualifying condition.

Source: Health Canada, Dutch Euthanasia Commission, Belgian Federal Commission

OBJECTION 6"This is a conspiracy theory."

Every number on this site comes from the Government of Canada.

  • 76,475 cumulative MAID deaths — Health Canada
  • 5.1% of all Canadian deaths — Health Canada
  • 47% loneliness, 49% burden — Health Canada
  • 2.42× overrepresentation from poorest quintile — Ontario Coroner / academic research
  • Veterans offered MAID — Parliamentary testimony, confirmed by Veterans Minister
  • Veteran suicide rates — Veterans Affairs Canada, DND
  • Disability backlog — Auditor General of Canada

This is not a conspiracy theory. This is arithmetic applied to government data. The only question is whether you will read the numbers the government published about itself.

OBJECTION 7"What does MAID have to do with whistleblower prosecution?"

Everything.

The foreign interference I reported was targeting the mental health support infrastructure that serves Canadian soldiers. The same soldiers who, when they leave service:

  • Die by suicide at 1.4–1.9× the civilian rate
  • Wait months for disability benefits (47% meeting standard)
  • Have 24% of mental health claims denied
  • Are offered MAID when they ask for a wheelchair ramp

When the system silences the person who reports a threat to soldiers' mental health, and then fails to provide mental health support to those same soldiers, and then offers them death instead — that is a connected system. The prosecution is not separate from the MAID crisis. It is part of the same institutional machinery that treats Canadian lives as disposable.
